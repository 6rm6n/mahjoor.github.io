starting a tutoring business

Arman Mahjoor

October 2nd, 2023


Throughout my life, I have had the opportunity to be involved with a variety of work, from farming, grading papers, canvassing voters, doing food deliveries, or answering coding questions online.

I would argue that white collar work is the one which I've had the most experience with, working at Kumon, Mathnasium, and as a CS TA @ GMU for the past 5 years. However each time I took on one of these roles, through the autonomous work I did and the sacrifice of time and commitment I took for these businesses, I started to question my compensation for my work to the actual worth I had.

My ambitious mindset sought after me when I decided I wanted to do tutoring on a contracting basis, signing up to tutor for a few companies on the internet. But at the time, I didn't really take action on any of these platforms and it wouldn't be until over a year later, when I received an automated text message from one of the platforms telling me I'd received a message from someone.
